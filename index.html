About Me

I am currently a doctoral researcher at ETH Zürich, with experience organizing diverse groups of people and solving complex problems with them.

My technical background in chemistry, computer science, and mathematics has prepared me to tackle problems using a data-driven and analytical approach. This is complemented by adaptability and communication skills which have developed from living in and travelling to over 30 countries.

Ultimately, my passion lies in understanding how complicated systems work and in implementing technologically relevant solutions to modern problems. In other words, I love to ask ‘Why?’ and I actively pursue projects which positively impact people’s lives!

My Experience

  • March 2017 - Present

    ETH Zürich

    Doctoral Researcher

    Development of Instanton Theory
    Developed a new theory for modeling chemical reaction rates in the atmosphere using the path-integral representation of quantum mechanics. Created a method which improves the computational efficiency of instanton theory by several orders of magnitude and extends its applicability to reactions at very low temperatures. Engineered software in Python using GitHub workflow and optimized code to run on a parallelized computer cluster.

  • September 2014 - December 2016

    SDSU/UCSD

    Graduate Researcher

    Computational Analysis of Hydrocarbon Combustion
    Investigated the dynamical properties of hydrocarbon molecules using statistical rate theories and state-of-the-art computational chemistry software. Provided theoretical and technical support in partnership with international research projects and published results in top academic journals. Managed 3 researchers and created an effective system for performing simultaneous calculations and compiling the resulting data.

  • August 2012 - July 2014

    MicroVention Inc.

    Research Associate

    Invention of a Liquid Embolic Medical Device
    Collaborated with an interdisciplinary group of researchers to invent a novel medical device used to treat vascular malformations in humans. Collected, analyzed, and clearly communicated scientific data in order to guide important business decisions. Regularly met with engineers, quality analysts, lawyers, and physicians to scale up the device and ultimately receive CE marking for sale in European markets.

  • July 2009 - July 2012

    UCI Football Club

    Vice-President

    Establishment of a Student-Run Football Club
    Organized a team of dedicated student athletes to serve as a board for the UC Irvine Men's Football Club. Increased both player turnout and club income by 200% over 3 years. Coordinated regional meetings and hosted large-scale tournaments with football clubs from all parts of the Southwestern United States. Managed player development by hosting both personal and team training sessions.
